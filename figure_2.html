Most suicides occur between ages 35-54 and 55-74. That should remain relatively consistant over time whereas the generational analysis will continue to evolve as younger generations reach that peak (35-55) age group. Unfortunately as the ages were already binned for us we are unable to split 35-34 into two to see how it would compare if it were a ten year age range instead of twenty. This is a limitation of the dataset we used. It is surprising that the 15-24 age group is number 4 of 7 as the most publicised suicides are in that age group. It is also surprising that the number of suicides in the 75+ is as high as it is.
